Time Management:
Capturing The Golden Deer

  • April 22, 2020
  • Soft Skills
Time and tide wait for none.

The proverb never gets old, rather we can feel it in the depth of our daily life, every day, in a new style. Attempts to hack time have been, are, and will continue to be. But the golden deer called 'time' was always out of our reach. Since there is no time machine, shouldn't we think of an alternative? Some magic to bring ‘time’ into our control?
Well, I have one - Time Management! Unlike abra-ka-dabra, this magic is not so easy to learn and adapt. But, I managed some hacks!

Time management is the method of planning and applying conscious control of time consumed on specific actions, especially to increase effectiveness, performance and productivity.
Time management skill allows us to achieve more in less time, which provides us with more free time. Having some free time daily is very important. It lets us take advantage of learning opportunities, lowers our stress, improves our focus and helps us to lead a more successful life. Each benefit of time management improves another aspect of our life.

11 Tips and Tricks to Hack Time :

Use a time management app

Using a good to-do app or task management app is one of the best ways to catch the golden deer of time management. Some great apps are -Google Tasks, Keep, Planner, Notion etc. To-do or task management app reminds you about the task, helps you to make a daily plan, and shows your productivity statistics. Some great apps which include Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning can also suggest when you should do a task, or if you need a break.

04 D's of Time Management

The 04 D's of time management are: DO - DELETE - DELEGATE - DEFER.
The 04 D helps us to know our task and provide us with a suggestion about when we should do that.
Do: When the task is important, and you are free. DO it!
Delete: When the task is not important, and you are not free either. DELETE the task!
Delegate: When the task is important, but you have no time to do it. DELEGATE the task!
Defer: When the task is not important, but you are free; think about the unimportant task and DEFER. What do you want to do now? Do you want to do an insignificant job or prepare yourself for the next important one?

Be Prepared For The Day Before It Starts

Update your to-do list for tomorrow before going to bed. Plan your day! Manage all the tasks you have according to priority and time. Thus, you will know what is coming ahead, and you'll be prepared.

Prioritize and Ignore

Judging thyself is a great way to start. Every night, before sleep, try to judge your day.
Ask - Did you complete all the tasks assigned for the day?
How productive was the day?
Which improvements do you need to make?

Put an Accurate Time-limit

Putting an accurate time limit will make you focus on your task and help you work more efficiently. A time limit will work as a guardian, will help you reach your goal and make you stay consistent. If your time limit breaks often, give it research! Learn your mistakes and why this is happening and solve accordingly.

Determine The Output

Before assigning yourself to a task, determine the output of the task. How will it benefit you? Why do you want to do the task? Why do you need it to be done? Have a clear purpose of doing the job. Having a clear purpose will keep you focused and energized.

Early to Bed, Early to Rise

Go to bed fast, not at midnight. Try to rise early in the morning. The sooner you start your day, the higher time you'll have to complete your tasks and give yourself some entertainment.
Besides this, morning is the best time for physical exercises and some meditation or yoga. These all will give you a boost of physical, mental and spiritual energy throughout the day.

Take Breaks, Power-Naps

Running all day around and doing things - is not time management. You have to take enough rest between tasks and provide yourself with some time and motivation to rock on. Have one or two good power nap or meditation sessions in a day. Sound mental and spiritual health is also essential for a perfect professional life.

Remove Distractions & Interruptions

Remove distractions while working, to get better focus and get the work done in time. Distractions are enemies. Stay out of Social Media while working, use apps like Forest to maintain focus.
Try to reduce the number of interruptions in your life. It is not a very easy thing to do, so if you are unable, try to alter the way in which you deal with them. Managing interrupts perfectly will help you make the best use of your day.

Unplanned Task: Friend or Enemy?

Everything doesn't go as planned always. Many unwanted things may happen, and many unwanted tasks may be assigned to you. But, never get upset about this. Rather than considering it your enemy, accept it as a challenge.

It is the best time to show your capabilities and skills of management and adaptation. Assess the importance and urgency of the new tasks, reschedule your routine, and complete them accordingly. Turn the pain into power!

Once you have mastered time, you will understand how true it is that most people overestimate what they can accomplish in a year - and underestimate what they can achieve in a decade!
